I gave an invited talk at the [Computational Math in Industry and Beyond Seminar Series (CME 500)](https://icme.stanford.edu/events/research-symposium-seminars/industry-applications-computational-and-mathematical-engineering) at the Institute for Computational and Mathematical Engineering (ICME) at Stanford University on our work on the development of time series foundation models, including Chronos and Chronos-Bolt. In particular, I discussed how subtle differences in their design choices can lead to inductive biases and performance differences on various time series datasets including chaotic systems. Please find my slides [here](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1Dctq-eVQG_QHkjsuJ2kXyomc5u3srVbH/edit?usp=sharing&ouid=112184455899852749897&rtpof=true&sd=true).
